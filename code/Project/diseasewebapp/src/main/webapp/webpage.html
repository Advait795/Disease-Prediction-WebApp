Service

Here, we provide specialized sections for Stroke, Diabetes, and Hypertension prediction, each offering tailored risk assessments, early detection tools, and personalized prevention strategies.

A health risk assessment form collects a comprehensive set of data to evaluate an individual's risk for various health conditions. It asks for demographic details such as sex and age, and specific health conditions like hypertension, heart disease, and smoking history. Key health metrics include BMI(Body Mass Index), HbA1c level (indicating average blood glucose over the past three months), and current blood glucose level. Additionally, the form gathers cardiovascular data, including chest pain type (cp), resting blood pressure (trestbps), cholesterol level (chol), fasting blood sugar (fbs), resting electrocardiographic results (restecg), maximum heart rate achieved (thalach), exercise-induced angina (exang),ST depression induced by exercise (oldpeak), slope of the peak exercise ST segment (slope), number of major vessels colored by fluoroscopy (ca), and thalassemia status (thal). Socioeconomic factors such as marital status, work type, residence type, and average glucose level are also considered. The form uses this detailed information to calculate risk scores for conditions like cardiovascular diseases and diabetes, allowing healthcare providers to identify high-risk individuals and develop targeted, personalized interventions.
